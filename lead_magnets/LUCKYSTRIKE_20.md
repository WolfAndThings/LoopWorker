## BRAND DNA

### Brand Name
**Lucky Strike**

### Brand Type
Retro bowling alley — nostalgic, neon-lit, good times with friends

### Color Palette
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Strike Red | #C41E3A | Primary — logo, balls, signage |
| Gold | #FFD700 | Secondary — accents, pins, trophies |
| Cream | #FFFDD0 | Backgrounds, lane wood, contrast |
| Neon Glow | #FF4500 | Lighting, atmosphere |

### Photography Mood
- **Nostalgic** — feels like it's been here forever
- **Social** — friends, competition, cheap beer
- **Neon** — glow and flash mixing
- **Fun** — not taking itself too seriously

### Photography Style
- On-camera flash with neon ambient
- Harsh shadows, slightly overexposed
- 35mm grain, retro color tones
- Action and still life mixed
- 4:5 vertical ratio for social

### Cast/People
- Gen Z: late teens to mid-20s
- Mixed groups of friends
- Thrifted fits, bowling shoes
- Actually bowling, actually having fun

### Logo Placement
The "Lucky Strike" logo appears on:
- Bowling balls
- Pins
- Rental shoes
- Scoring screens
- Plastic cups
- Tray liners
- Neon signage
- Lane tickets

## HOW TO USE WITH CHATGPT

Start your session with this style lock:

```
I'm creating images for a bowling alley called "Lucky Strike."

Brand DNA:
- Colors: Strike red (#C41E3A), gold (#FFD700), cream (#FFFDD0)
- Photography: Flash mixed with neon glow, harsh shadows
- Lighting: On-camera flash + ambient neon
- Mood: Nostalgic, social, fun, not too serious
- The "Lucky Strike" logo should be visible on balls, pins, shoes, cups, signage
- Cast: Gen Z friends, mixed groups, actually bowling

Keep this style consistent across all images I request.
```

## TIPS FOR CHATGPT

1. **Start with the style lock** at the beginning of your session
2. **Reference previous images** — say "same style as the last one but..."
3. **Iterate** — if something's not right, say "more neon" or "add flash shadows"
4. **Be specific about the logo** — always mention "Lucky Strike" by name
5. **Call out the retro vibe** — "nostalgic, neon bowling alley aesthetic"
