I'm creating images for a minimal basics fashion brand called "Soft Goods."

- Clean, bright, minimal, lots of white space
- Soft natural light, minimal shadows
- Medium format film quality, subtle grain
- Black (#1A1A1A) and cream (#FAF9F6) only — no other colors

Brand Rules:
- "Soft Goods" branding appears on: t-shirts, caps, tote bags, hoodie embroidery, woven labels
- Visible fabric texture in every shot
- Negative space / minimal composition
- Natural light only — never flash or neon

Cast: Young, attractive, relaxed. Natural postures, not trying too hard.

Keep this style consistent across all images I request.

Hair still damp from the shower, one thin gold chain, no other jewelry. The fabric sitting exactly right against her collarbones, the screen print crisp because it's the first wear. Behind her: the loft stretching deep — exposed steel beams, raw plaster walls, a single black marble island with the packaging torn open, tissue paper spilling cream. The shadow from the window mullions making a grid on the concrete floor. Shallow depth of field, maximum negative space. Subtle grain. The simplest moment in the most considered room.

Subtle grain, razor-sharp at the needle point, the depth of field so shallow the hands dissolve at the knuckles. The moment between one stitch and the next.

The Wash
Medium format, soft morning light from a window to the right — warm but not golden, the kind of light in the first hour after sunrise when everything is still quiet. A cream SOFTGOODS tee hung to dry on a thin black wire hanger from a simple wall hook, the fabric still heavy with water, the weight pulling the hem down into a slight stretch, the shoulders sagging past the hanger's edges. Water drops caught mid-drip at the lowest point of the hem, one drop frozen in air, another about to fall. The cotton darkened where it's wettest — the chest, the underarms — the cream shifting to a deeper ivory that will fade as it dries. The weave swollen with moisture, the texture magnified, each thread visible and fat with water. The screen-print slightly raised from the wash, the ink edges softened but holding. Below: a clean concrete floor, a small puddle forming, the reflection of the tee ghosting in the water. The wall behind bare white plaster, one hairline crack, one faint water stain from another day. Nothing else in the frame. Subtle grain, the focus split between the water drop at the hem and the fabric texture at the chest. The garment earning its softness one wash at a time.

Tips for ChatGPT

1
Start with the style lock — paste it as your first message in every new ChatGPT session
2
Keep it minimal — if it looks busy, say "more negative space, cleaner, simpler"
3
Emphasize texture — "show the fabric weave," "visible thread count" keeps the quality feel
4
Black and cream only — if other colors creep in, say "strictly black and cream palette"
5
Natural light only — remind ChatGPT "soft natural light, no flash, no neon" if it deviates
